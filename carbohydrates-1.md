# Carbohydrates - 1

## What are Carbohydrates?

- Carbohydrates contain carbon, hydrogen and oxygen and are represented by the formula $C_m(H_2O)_n$ or $(CH_2)_n$
- Modern definition: Carbohydrates are the polyhydroxy aldehydes or ketones and their derivates.
- Carbohydrates are the primary product of photosynthesis.
- They are also known as hydrates of carbon.

## Functions of Carbohydrates

- It is a source of energy. It provides 4kcal/g. Glucose fuels the work of most of the body's cells. Excess glucose is stored as glycogen.
- An inadequate supply of glucose would result in the breakdown of proteins to synthesize glucose. So, carbohydrates prevent proteins from being broken down.
- It helps to prevent ketosis. Carbohydrates arev required for the complete metabolism of fat. Incomplete fat metabolism form ketones.

## Classification of Carbohydrates

- Carbohydrates are classified depending upon the number of sugar units.

### Monosaccharides

- Monosaccharides are simple sugars consist of a single polyhydroxy aldehyde or ketone unit which cannot be further hydrolysed.
- Aldoses- contain aldehyde group.
- Ketoses- contain ketone group.
- Depending upon the total number of carbon atoms they are called
  - Trioses - 3 carbon atoms
  - Tetroses- 4 carbon atoms
  - Pentoses- 5 carbon atoms
  - Hexoses- 6 carbon atoms
- The simplest sugars are - glyceraldehyde and dihydroxyacetone.
- Most abundant monosaccharide in the nature is glucose.
- Aldose has carbonyl on carbon-1 and ketose has on carbon-2.
- Almost all naturally occurring monosaccharides belong to D-series
- D-glucose is both an aldehyde and a polyalcohol. It is a hexose.
- Ketose sugars always end with 'ulose'. The exceptions are fructose and dihydroxyacetone.
  - D-fructose is an example. It makes up 55% of high fructose corn syrup and 40% of honey.
- Carbohydrates are represented by 3 types of formulae
  - Fischer projection
  - Haworth cyclic structure
  - Conformational formula
